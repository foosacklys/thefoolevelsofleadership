Level 2 Competency Model

Making Good Life & Financial Decisions
  • Makes Responsible Life Decisions
    • Stays out of unnecessary drama, habits or distractions
      “I’ve had to say no to some things lately — I’m focused on growing and showing up right.”
    • Managers their schedule to get rest, work, and life in balance.
      “I changed my weekend habits so I’m sharper when I lead Saturday opens.”
    • Handles transportation, housing and family responsibility with consistency.
  • Manages Their Finances With Maturity
    • Lives within their means, budgets and saves regularly.
      "I set up automatic savings and stopped eating out so much — I’m trying to get ahead"
    • Doesn’t miss shifts or underperform due to money stress.
    • Seeks and applies sound financial advice.
      "“I talked to someone about budgeting — it’s already helped me feel more in control."
  • Builds a Stable Foundation for Leadership
    • Keeps personal stress from spilling onto the team.
    • Uses tools and routines to stay grounded.
      “I finally built a real morning routine. It’s made a huge difference in how I start shift.”
      “I’ve been prioritizing sleep and workouts — it helps me lead with clarity.”
Self-Awareness & Integrity
  • Owns Their Growth and Mindset
    • Seeks out feedback without getting defensive.
      “I’ve been thinking about how I communicated yesterday. I can do better.”
    • Reads, reflects, and brings those learnings to shift.
    • Works to grow where they’re weak — not just where they’re comfortable.
      “I started keeping a list of what I learn after each shift — it helps me stay intentional.”
  • Models Honesty and Integrity
    • Speaks truthfully, even when it’s uncomfortable.
      “That was on me. I told the team the wrong thing — I’ve already corrected it.”
    • Acknowledges mistakes without deflection.
      “I’m not going to blame them — I could’ve communicated better from the start.”
    • Speaks well of the company and team, even off the clock.
  • Leads With Self-Control
    • Regulates tone and body language under pressure.
      “I’m frustrated, but I’m not going to pass that on to the crew.”
    • Resets their own energy before leading others.
      “Let’s reset. We’re not where we want to be, but we can still finish strong.”
    • Doesn’t let personal emotion leak into the shift.
Maintains Personal Organization & Preparedness
  • Comes Prepared and Ready
    • Shows up early, sharp, and focused.
      "I get here early to think through the team and flow of the shift."
    • Brings everything they need (proper uniform & clean attire, tools, notes, mindset).
    • Has already mentally walked the shift before clocking in.
      “I use my drive in to plan how I want the shift to feel and who I’m working with.”
  • Uses Systems to Stay on Top
    • Has a calendar, planner, or written system — not just memory.
      “Every Sunday night I plan out the week — it makes the shift easier.”
    • Keeps their areas of responsibilities well managed, executes checklists and keeps personal notes organized.
    • Manages their own schedule like they expect the team to.
Follows-Through to Followthrough
  • Finishes What They Start
    • Doesn’t walk past things or leave tasks halfway done.
    • Gets the shift to the finish line — even if it takes staying late.
      "We were behind, so I stayed until we caught up and set up for tomorrow."
      “I checked everything before close using the checklist — even double checked the restrooms and lobby.”
    • Communicates if they need help before dropping the ball.
  • Takes Ownership of the Whole Shift
    • Owns opening, lunch rush, prep, close, quality, and customer outcomes.
    • Doesn’t just report problems — works to fix them.
      "I handled it with the customer, coached the team, and left a note for the manager."
    • Leads with “I got it” energy, not excuses.
      “I couldn’t find the build-to list so I did a quick rebuild from scratch and trained the opener.”
  • Builds Trust Through Consistency
    • You can count on them. They show up, follow up, and close loops.
    • They don’t disappear when things get tough — they lean in.
    • Other leaders trust them with hard things.
      "I’ll take care of it and follow up with you later today."
People Skills & Culture Building
  • Coaches with Care and Clarity
    • Gives feedback directly — not with sarcasm or side talk.
      "I’m bringing it up because I believe in and know your potential."
    • Balances praise and correction as part of their daily rhythm.
      “I coached her in the moment — not in front of everyone”
    • Teaches why the standard matters.
  • Builds Trust and Integrity with the Team
    • Shows up the same way, no matter who’s watching.
    • Apologizes when needed.
      "That was on me yesterday. I should’ve been more clear up front.”
  • Protects the Culture
    • Redirects negativity or unhelpful language.
      "We’re not doing that today — let’s keep it positive and locked in."
    • Encourages the quieter voices on the team.
      “I could tell he was bothered by that comment that Tim made, so I stepped in to help redirect and improve the conversation”
    • Models fun and focus.
Training and The 5-Step Process
  • Uses a Clear Training Method
    • Applies the full 5-Step Training Process.
      “I modeled, mentored and monitored—when I saw they were having trouble I stepped back to mentor again.”
    • Motivates trainees
      “You’re doing it! That’s exactly the right way. Keep it up!”
    • Multiplies by training employees to a level of understanding that they can eventually train.
    • Tracks progress, gives updates, and doesn’t wing it.
    • Understands what “graduation” looks like before moving on.
      “She’s solid on Server 2 technicals, but I’m working on her confidence on answering customer questions and would like to see a little more ‘Leap To Serve’.”
  • Teaches Mindset, Not Just Tasks
    • Explains the why — not just the how.
      “The reason we are focusing on ‘Leaping To Serve’ is because we want to WOW every customer that walks through those doors!”
    • Uses in-the-moment coaching.
      “I asked him to slow down and focus on quality — then we coached it out during dinner rush.”
  • Trains Even When Not The Trainer
    • Offers helpful reminders in real time and after shifts.
      “I gave him some feedback about the Assembly shift before he left for the day—he was doing well, but the presentation and portioning was a little off during our peak business.”
    • Coaches in small moments: handoffs, walk-bys, etc.
      “She was flustered on Server 1, so I stood nearby and talked her through the next 3 guests.”
    • Looks out for new team members and helps them feel confident.
    • Never says “not my job” when someone needs help learning.
      “Even though I wasn’t the trainer, I followed up and asked how her close went — then gave one quick tip.”
  • Invests in Future Leaders
    • Sees who’s ready for more — and who needs more time.
      "I think she’s ready for the next step, but I’d like to be on the next shift just to make sure.”
    • Encourages people into growth zones.
      “I gave him a stretch goal on Toast & Fries and helped him through the shift to achieve it.”
    • Raises their expectations as people improve.
System Ownership (Captainships)
  • Carries Their Role With Excellence
    • Has clear accountability over a specific area (FOH Trainer, Facility Captain, Sauce Master, etc.).
      “I’ve updated the facility cleaning checklist — last week we missed 3 items.”
    • Knows what good looks like — and resets when needed.
    • Tracks trends and looks for improvement.
      “I’ve noticed we’ve been cutting it close on batter every order. I need to look at yields but more importantly, we also seem to be wasting a lot of batter in our PM shifts.”
  • Trains and Coaches Others in the System
    • Coaches peers when things slip.
    • Teaches new team members how to do it right.
      “I noticed sauce cups were not as full and so took some time today to model the proper technique with the morning prep team.”
    • Doesn’t wait on a manager to reset standards
  • Communicates Proactively
    • Updates managers on wins, issues, and ideas.
      “I’ve got an idea to make this easier to keep up with — want to see it?”
      “I left a Slack note about what I adjusted — let me know if it needs tweaking.”
    • Doesn’t wait until things are broken to speak up.
    • Takes pride in making their system stronger.
Communication & Presence
  • Speaks With Clarity and Confidence
    • Gives short, direct shift instructions
      “Let’s get food down and get prepared for tablet.”
    • Communicates expectations in real time.
      “Let’s check tea, ice, lemonade and restrooms before we get busy. We’re about to see some business today!”
  • Leads the Room With Their Energy
    • Stays calm under pressure.
      “We’ve got this, it’s just one ticket at a time.”
    • Doesn’t yell or talk down — stays composed.
    • Brings enthusiasm and energy for every shift whether managing or working stations.
    • Uses tone to set the emotional tone of the shift.
      “Head on a swivel — let’s stay locked in and finish strong.”
  • Communicates With the Bigger Picture in Mind
    • Stays connected to leadership convos and priorities.
      “I updated it in Slack after close — we caught up on sauce and coached Jordan on proper FIFO.”
    • Brings information back to the team with clarity.
      “Let me explain what’s changing and why — so it doesn’t catch us off guard.”
    • Uses Slack or handoff notes to communicate professionally.
Brand Advocacy & Stewardship
  • Represents the Brand With Pride
    • Speaks positively about the company in public and private.
      “I always tell people — we don’t just serve food, we serve people.”
    • Wears the uniform like a leader — clean, sharp, ready.
    • Models our values on and off the floor.
      “I wear the brand like I mean it — even when I’m just grabbing coffee.”
  • Takes Ownership Beyond the Shift
    • Looks for ways to help improve the restaurant or systems.
      “I saw something that might help the open run smoother — want to test it?”
    • Gives upward feedback respectfully.
    • Hi-lights patterns or inconsistencies.
      “We’ve missed three days on the the time change sheet — I’ll follow up and retrain.”
    • Takes responsibility for their store — not just their shift.
      “That cooler keeps getting left open — I’m coaching it every shift now.”
      “We’ve got a system that could scale if we write it down — want help with that?”
  • Protects Company Resources
    • Respects the food, tools, and materials they’re responsible for.
      “Hey y’all — we’re wasting too many cups. Let’s get that under control.”
    • Encourages the team to treat the business the same way.
    • Looks for long-term fixes, not shortcuts.
  • Inspires Others to Lead Too
    • Encourages teammates to take the next step.
      “I told her she’d be great at training — she just needed someone to believe in her first.”
    • Celebrates leadership wins — even small ones.
      “You led Assembly today like a pro. Keep pushing — you’ve got this.”
    • Helps make leadership feel accessible and worth it.
